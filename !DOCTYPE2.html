"Do Not Use" List Of Abbreviations

I have put together a list of medical abbreviations that should not be used. These abbreviations are likely to be mixed up with other abbreviations and cause confusion between patient care providers.

Do not use:

  • U or I Rationale: It can be confused with "0," "4," or "cc". Write out "unit" instead.

  • IU Rationale: It can be confused with "IV" or "10". Write out "International Unit" instead.

  • Q.D., QD, q.d., qd Rationale: They can be confused with each other or with "00" or "I". Write out "daily" instead.

  • Q.O.D., QOD, q.o.d., qod Rationale: They can be confused with each other or with "00" or "I". Write out "every other day" instead.

  • MS Rationale: It can mean either magnesium sulfate or morphine sulfate. Instead, write out "magnesium sulfate" or "morphine sulfate".

  • MSO4, MgSO4 Rationale: They can be confused with each other. Write out "magnesium sulfate" for safety.

  • Training zeros when writing numbers with decimals Rationale: They can be confused with adding zeros to a number (ex. "1.0" vs "10"). Do not include a trailing zero (ex. write "1" instead of "1.0").

  • Leading zero is missing when writing numbers with decimals Rationale: They can be confused with a different number (ex. ".2" vs "2"). Always use a leading zero (ex. write "0.2" instead of ".2")
